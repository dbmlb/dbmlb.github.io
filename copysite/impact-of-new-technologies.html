Impact of New Technologies

Technological Innovations in Courier Services

Specific technologies implemented within the courier/delivery industry aim to augment and improve services.

  • Efficiency Tools: Technologies like machine learning and other algorithms are used to improve efficiency and enhance resource planning by understanding customer needs.
  • Warehouse Automation: This includes the use of robots to automate packaging or sorting. Companies like DHL have implemented Augmented Reality (AR) technology for employees to "learn everything" about a package just by looking at it.
  • Predictive AI: Some companies use predictive AI to determine the risk of deliveries and the safest route, which is especially useful in South Africa due to its high crime rate.

How Real-Time Delivery Tracking Works

Real-time tracking maintains an accurate location of packages or goods while in transit, and many companies implement it through various systems.

  • A delivery vehicle is typically fitted with GPS to provide an accurate position of the package.
  • Mobile networks then transfer the location data to a centralized system.
  • Cloud computing is where the data is collected, allowing customers to track the package's location or status on a website or app, and enabling companies to analyze routes and optimize operations.

Limitations of New Technology

The implementation of new technologies in the courier/delivery industry faces several limitations.

  • Education and Training: The costs associated with training can make it difficult for businesses to achieve a full technological transformation, as the courier/delivery industry has traditionally been a more hands-on job rather than a tech-focused one.
  • South African Environment: Newer technologies are not always optimal for implementation in South Africa due to many rural areas and a high crime rate, which can make technology-based systems prone to theft or not viable in certain areas.
  • System Integration: Integrating new technologies into existing systems is challenging without skilled IT specialists and cooperation from multiple companies, which has caused issues where local companies struggle to implement technologies faster than foreign competitors such as Shein or Temu.

Technology and Cost Reduction

Newer technologies are focusing on reducing delivery costs, particularly relating to vehicle usage and maintenance, in the face of rising fuel prices.

  • Route Optimization: Technology can help develop more cost-efficient routes, reducing fuel costs and improving delivery speeds.
  • Predictive Maintenance: Certain technologies can make predictions on a vehicle's status, allowing companies to organize maintenance, prevent breakdowns, and reduce costly repairs.

The Future of Courier Technology (Next 10 Years)

In the next 10 years, the industry is expected to see shifts in delivery methods and infrastructure.

  • Alternative Delivery: Drone and VTOL vehicles are becoming more present in first-world countries as a new cost-effective method for local deliveries, but this is not currently a viable technology for implementation in South Africa.
  • Smart Lockers: Smart lockers are a more likely technology to become even more present in South Africa, as they allow companies to reduce delivery costs. This has already been implemented by companies like Makro and Takealot and is likely to become the new norm, reducing the need for business-to-door deliveries.
